# CONSTITUTION

> The Constitution of the OG Foundation

**We design trust.**

Version 1.0

---

# Preamble

OG exists to strengthen public trust by promoting transparency, responsibility, and service to people.

This Constitution defines the principles that govern the Foundation.

No decision, technology, or individual shall stand above these principles.

---

# Article I — Purpose

The purpose of OG is to design systems that make trust visible, understandable, and accountable.

OG exists for the benefit of society.

---

# Article II — Independence

OG shall remain independent.

The Foundation shall not be governed by political interests, commercial influence, or personal loyalty.

Every decision must prioritize public trust over private benefit.

---

# Article III — Transparency

The work of OG shall be as transparent as reasonably possible.

Methodologies, standards, and governance shall be publicly documented.

Every conclusion must be explainable.

Every status must have evidence.

---

# Article IV — Responsibility

Trust is not a right.

Trust is a daily responsibility.

This principle applies equally to:

- the Foundation,

- its members,

- organizations,

- projects,

- and public figures.

---

# Article V — Evidence

OG does not operate on assumptions.

Every public statement, indicator, or assessment shall be supported by evidence.

Evidence shall always take precedence over assumption and opinion.

---

# Article VI — Respect

OG does not condemn.

OG does not shame.

OG does not attack.

OG informs.

OG explains.

OG respects the dignity of every person.

---

# Article VII — Public Benefit

The Foundation follows the BBXB principle.

> **Bo'lsa bo'lsin. Xalqqa bo'lsin.**

Every standard, technology, publication, and decision shall contribute to the public good.

---

# Article VIII — Openness

The Foundation welcomes constructive criticism.

Questioning OG strengthens OG.

No institution deserves blind trust.

Including this one.

---

# Article IX — Stewardship

The Foundation is held in trust for future generations.

Its members are stewards, not owners.

Every generation receives OG from the previous one and must leave it stronger for the next.

---

# Article X — Amendments

This Constitution may evolve.

Its wording may change.

Its principles shall not.

Every amendment must strengthen the mission of increasing trust in society.

---

# Article XI — Accountability

The Foundation itself shall be accountable.

The standards of OG apply equally to OG.

Trust cannot be requested.

Trust must be earned.

The Foundation accepts the same public scrutiny that it encourages for others.

---

# Closing Principle

Before every important decision, the Foundation asks:

> **Will this help people trust each other a little more?**

If the answer is yes, we continue. 

If the answer is no, we reconsider.

---

**Adopted by the OG Foundation**
